Scam Alerts

Common scams targeting tourists and how to avoid them

Fake Gemstone Scam

Sellers claim gemstones are valuable and offer to ship them internationally for profit

Prevention tips:
Avoid expensive gemstones from street vendors
Don't trust 'export business' offers
Research prices before purchasing

Taxi Meter Manipulation

Drivers claim broken meters or quote fixed high prices

Prevention tips:
Insist on using the meter
Know approximate distances and costs
Use ride-sharing apps when available

Fake Charity Donations

People approach tourists asking for donations to fake orphanages or schools

Prevention tips:
Research legitimate charities beforehand
Don't give money to unsolicited requests
Visit organizations directly if interested

General Safety Tips

Always research prices beforehand and negotiate when appropriate
Trust your instincts - if something feels too good to be true, it probably is
Connect with local guides through reputable agencies
Keep copies of important documents and emergency contacts
